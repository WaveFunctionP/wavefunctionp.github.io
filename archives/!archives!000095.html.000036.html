Relief for Your Computing Obsession

Anybody who spends a signifigant time behind the computer will agree that often times it can be painful. Many people that spend thier waking hours typing on the keyboard have found that they start losing feeling in their hands and start experiencing pains away from the computer. Common tasks like opening a door become a dreaded affair because of the painful effect. Carpal Tunnet Syndrome (CTS) has gotten plenty of meadia attention. Still many don't know why they are experiencing pain, although there are many effective and inexpensive treatments that could aleviate most of the symptoms. Ars has a review up about the IMAK Smart Glove, a product to help protect your wrist from further injury while allowing them to heal.

Many people have this problem, but a few have a different problem called Cubital Tunnel Syndrom. The main difference in symptoms is that the numbness primarily occurs on the ring and pinky finger. It is caused by inflamation on a nerve that travels through the elbow as apposed to the wrist. The most effective treatment is simple to not rest your elbow on anything to relieve the pressure and inflamation. Along with freaquent breaks, many see full recovery.

I can testify to it simply because I have this problem. When at it's worst I could hardly use my right hand for anything, it was simply to weak and the pain was unbearable. I had tried all the techniques for relief for CTS, but they weren't effective. I finally went to the doctor and he informed me of CuTS. It took me a while to get used to not resting my elbow as I typed, but after a while I became more aware of my elbow position. I also took frequent breaks away from the keyboard to straighten my arm. After about 3 weeks my pain went away and, after about 3 months, I recieved full feeling back to my hand. There is relief to these problems, but it requires a little change to your routine. The change was well worth it though. Don't forget to talk to your doctor about it.
