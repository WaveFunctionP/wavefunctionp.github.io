Bush's Misguided Advisor

Life extension has a new highly influential foe. Meet Leon Kass, Bush's bioethics council and he's often seen parading around advocating death. Yes, he wants you to die:

"The promise of immortality and eternity answers rather to a deep truth about the human soul: the human soul yearns for, longs for, aspires to some condition, some state, some goal toward which our earthly activities are directed but which cannot be attained in earthly life," says Kass. "The human taste for immortality, for the imperishable and the eternal, is not a taste that the biomedical conquest of death could satisfy. We would still be incomplete; we would still lack wisdom; we would still lack God's presence and redemption."
Mr. Kass, I hope you don't intend to impose your religious beliefs on me. I, for one, don't require death for spiritual enlightenment. I think many of you feel the same way. I know I'd like the chance to meet my great great great grandchildren.

Now keep in mind this is all speculation and we still have a long way to go if we get there at all, but it provides a very motivational goal. We'll just have to wait and see how this nanotechnology "revolution" will pan out.
