Will The RIAA See To It's own Demise?

The RIAA with all it spin doctoring and bogus claims have still yet to show any benefit for the music industry. TechDirt and The Register have reported on some of the evil organizations failed tactics to change public opinion. Despite all it's legal firepower, it has yet to show why it's "evidence" is valid. I remember reading an article about a recent raid that uncovered 420 or so cd burners, which after careful srutiny, amounted to ~150 high speed burners. Thats quite an exageration and the public at large is noticing, not to mention the legislature.

Latest theory: The RIAA will colapse under it's own weight.

I have a feeling that when everything is said and done. 5 or 10 years from now, there will be no public ear for the RIAA if they continue to push their bloated claims. I'm almost obliged to ignore all future RIAA press releases. Simply because to me their cries and moans are just the die throes of a mighty beast. I'm now fairly confident everything will work out in the end given time for the content industry to remodel their business model to a more progressive form. forget all the concern over user rights and piracy raising prices. They'll be short lived if they even happen at all. I have a feeling that RIAA won't last that long.
