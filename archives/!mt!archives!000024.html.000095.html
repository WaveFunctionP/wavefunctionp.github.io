Digital Freedom under fire?

Considering all the discussion about civil rights after 9/11, you would think that any civil rights resticting bills proposed by congress would be scrutinized more than usual, especially when "national security" is not a factor.
I found an intresting website listing some "badboy" bills that have been passed or are up for consideration.American Open Technology Consortium: The Worst Coders in Washington even has a list of legislators that have shown support for some of these bills. They even included campain contribution amounts from technology evil types ("Hollywood").
It's sad to see that one of my representatives on the list (Chip Pickering) at the top of the list no less. Most interesting, considering Ms lost a seat in Congress after the last census and "Chippy" is, or was, in a rather heated battle for the election this season. My old stand by man, Trent Lott, is not on the list though thank good!
Personally, the ones that concern me the most are DMCA, CIPA, and the"Berman P2P bill" (that gut most be on crack, thinking to legalizing vigilanteism on the net). DMCA will hopefully be neutered some next session with a couple of proposed bills to provide copyright liscensee protection. Ironic considering we had those rights before DMCA. CIPA is just wrong, any kind of censoring in the schools and libraries, direct or otherwise, makes a dangerous precident on what the government can force the public to see. (or not see) Before long, history books will be reading like there was no civil right movement or that Clinton was never involved with anything to do with cigars.
Another rant, I know, but I just can't keep this stuff in. It's just not healthy.
