100th Anniversary of Quantum Physics

Could someone please explain to me how it's been 100 years today from Dec 14, 1900? /. is reporting that it's the 100th anniversary of QP, but the math still has eluded me. The discussion is worth mentioning; it's absolutely hilarious!

Still it's hard to believe that QP has been going for so long. I wonder when the next revolution is going to start. Given our current rate of discovery, I wouldn't think that it should be too long until we have another theory to topple this one. Of course the new would have to topple not only QP, but the whole of the standard model. And somehow, I don't think it'll be accepted unless it incorporates the whole as one concise formula preferrably less than 3 inches in length. Kinda picky those physicists types...yep, real picky they are...
