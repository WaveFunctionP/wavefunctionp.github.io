The Web now has a Voice

In perhaps one of the most disturbing things I've ever heard, you can hear the voice of the web. But really it's more than that; the site collects conversations on various forums and chat rooms and combines them statisticly to produce a spoken soundscape. It uses different pitches and tones to mark new topics and indicate general traffic intensity.

The following from the web site:

What does the collective voice of the internet sound like? Countless others are with you when you browse the web, some reading the same words at the same time, and yet you have no way of sensing their presence. Listening Post gives voice to this vast, silent world, transforming collective online activity and communication into a multi-layered sound installation. This uniquely designed space monitors thousands of online exchanges in real time, revealing the patterns and rhythms of people communicating with each other.
The result it quite unnerving in my opinion. It made me real uneasy to listen to it, but it probably had something to do with the way they chose the voice and sound patterns. I couldn't connect to the Bell Labs site at the moment. (It's been /.ed) Nevertheless, I'm a changed man after hearing it. I don't think I'll ever get it out of my head.

But don't let that stop you from checking it out. Maybe I just caught it at the wrong moment. (I'm not sure if it changes or if it is just an example.) I need a drink....
