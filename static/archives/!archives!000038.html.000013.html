Deja Vu

Court "Overturns Limits on Wiretaps to Combat Terror", one of the many headlines so familiar since 9/11, rings a familiar tone in the ears of many. Bygone periods in U.S. history where civil rights, so adamantly fought for by our "forefathers" (at least that's the route popular history likes to take), were so abruptly cast away in a fury and paranoia of public sentiment. Perhaps McCarthyism, didn't teach America the lesson it sould have, and we are destined for a refresher course. Undoubtedly, we will take it willingly; trusting our gut feelings about what makes this country so great. Patriotism (read: Propaganda) is the fuel to the fire. It was also Nazi Germany's fuel of choice, as well as our's during WWII. Complete with our own version of prison camp. National security being of supreme importance, even above that of the very people our country is suposed to protect.

I thinks it's going to take some time for all this stuff to sink in. Still, I think i'm going to be very nautious before everything is said and done. I love this country as much as the next guy, but enough is enough. I feel as if I've steped into some sort of "Twilight Zone". Needless to say, I don't like it and I'm waiting for the credits.
