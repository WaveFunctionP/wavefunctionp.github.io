DNA = Fractal?

A patent has been filed by Andras Pellionisz, who expects to be able describe "junk" DNA as fractal patern.

Fractals are a way to describe complex geometric structures from one formula. Sort of like the way you can descible a circle with out even having seen that particular one, simply by means of a formula. It has been speculated that DNA's pattern is seems strikingly similar to that of a fractals. Pellionisz's hopes process would allow for a way to understand the "junk DNA's" purpose. As, Intron, the "junk DNA", is much more abundant than the more useful portions called "genes".

It's interesting to see biology being put a more rigorous mathematical footing. If this trend continues it could be very beneficial to biology's growth. Just look what math did for physics. Without a standard and univerally understood language, physics would be nowhere near where it is today. It's amasing to think how flexible numbers are at describing the world. It's mind-blowing that something so simple can even begin to describe what we see around us everyday.

But, then again, I've been told that I think too much.
