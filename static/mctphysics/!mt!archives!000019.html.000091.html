Physics for your ol' man

I went to Jackson the other day with my Dad to pick up some parts for my computer. We were talking and somehow we got on the subject of why I want to study physics. Of particular intrest to him was why I would read about a subject most people dread taking in college. Nevermind studying it for fun!
(I know I'm a geek. It's cool.)

I soon realized just how hard it is to explain physics. I tried explaining some of the features of superstring theory and a few particle interations. Before it was over, I probably covered each subject like 3 times. But even after he had a suitable understanding of what I was saying, he still couldn't understand what was so exciting. Nevermind what was so desirable about symmetry!

I think it's akin to trying to get someone excited about baseball when they are not familiar with the game, nor any sport for that matter.

Maybe I should have used the "two slits" experiment or explained boiling "watched" celcium atoms and all the wierdness that insues. Perhaps that would have pique his interest. Nevertheless, I've given up on trying to talk about it with my family. I guess I'll have to wait till I get into school to have an informed discusion.
