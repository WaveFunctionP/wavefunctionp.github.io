Speed Of Gravity Measured...Surprise!, Einstein Was Right

Hey, what news! Einstein was right. Scientists have measured the speed of gravity using the movement of Jupiter into the lightpath of a quasar. By knowing the mass of Jupiter, corrections were taken into account for the bending of light that should occur and observing the movement of light as Jupiter passed in front of the quasar. As Jupiter's gravity bent the light as it passed, measurements were made to check the speed that the gravity influenced the light passing nearby. Final numbers came to about 95% of the speed of light with an error of 25%, so it was fairly accurate. This confirms Einstein's prediction in General Relativity that gravity propagates at the same rate as light and further fueling the speculation that gravity is akin to light in a very fundamental way. Hello, supersymmetry.

I wish I knew what they were talking about when the say it disproves many newer theories the involve many dimensions. Superstring theory, in all it's various flavors, is probably included on that list. Supposedly, gravity could "shortcut" it's way through space utilizing these extra dimensions, but I assume it's just a pretty mathematics. Some kind of numerical voodoo that had nothing to do with physical observations. But such is the case without declining budgets given to physics departments nowadays. Only time will tell what effect that will have on economic development here in the US. Enough ranting, this is a great development.

Apple Making Headlines...With Me At Least

Steve Job's keynote at the MacWorldExpo has been creating quite a stir. Lust inspiring laptops, better browsers and a slew of software being released for the Mac. Now I'm firmly in the PC camp, but sometimes, especially as of late, I doubt my non-Unix upbringing. Apple's marketing campaign has begun to thoroughly work it's way into my head. I have to say that I've been thoroughly tempted into at least trying a Mac. Now I know what you are saying. "It's a phase; it will pass". I know, but they're so cute. When I saw the iPod, I immediately began to regret buying my Nomad Jukebox. But that's no big deal. The problem is that I keep coming across Mac only applications, that I really wish I could use. Top that with the fact that I'm just sick and tired of fiddling with every aspect of the PC. Using the PC is a chore, and one that I'm finding myself having a harder and harder time justifying.

The grass is almost always greener on the other side. Yes, I've used that argument too. To no great effect I might add. It seem every time I turn around I'm bombarded by "bad design". I'm not only talking about the technology in PCs, but aesthetics, maybe I'm getting soft, but I'd like an app to look as well as it performs. It even seems as if the latter is getting harder to find also. No one seems to be taking the time and effort the is need to ensure their product is finished before they release it. From my experience, when something attains a cult like following it at least warrants a passing glance. I too often find myself staring instead. I've been seduced by the Mac community. Given time I'm sure that I'll buy one. Most likely when I go looking for college lifestyle functionality. That will not be any time soon unless I secure a surplus of funds.Still, that 12" Powerbook looks sweet. Until that time I can only look on as a spectator....a dreadfully envious spectator.
